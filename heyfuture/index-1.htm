HeyFuture!

At the start of each year, young adults are asked to work through multiple options regarding their future including heading to Uni? getting a job? starting a business? taking a gap year? an apprenticeship? following a passion?

The backdrop of rapid technological advances, digital integration, the emergence of new sectors and the demise of traditional industries is creating real uncertainty on what path is the right one and young people are having to make these decisions in the absence of real knowledge or understanding. Parents are often unable to offer appropriate advice as they too are witnessing the disintegration of traditional skill sets in their workplace. 


Businesses are also driving change as they look for young people who are more adaptable, technical, creative and able to work in collaborative teams across a wide range of projects, in order to solve contemporary challenges.

These skills are not always learned in the classroom and generally need to be developed and nurtured if young adults are to build the capability, knowledge and insights for their road ahead.   

To respond to these needs The Mind Lab has developed a course specifically for 18- 22-year-olds who are deciding on their next steps. This could include study options or evaluating how to develop the skills to gain employment or to develop the digital skills needed to actively contribute in the new digital economy. 

Students on the HeyFuture! course will tackle the big lofty questions of their generation while building knowledge and capability in tech skills, soft skills (collaboration, creativity, curiosity etc) problem-solving, perseverance and ‘getting stuff done’.

Over the three-week course students will work on live industry projects, identifying opportunities and creating solutions while developing real-world skills including project management, digital skills, communication and problem-solving. They will learn to use contemporary tools of business. They will also gain insight on how to create an online profile to create networks and find jobs.

Course Components

What does the future hold?

An in-depth insight into the impact of technological change, climate change, globalisation, and how this shapes the future of work

The importance of personal purpose and how to find yours as you navigate your next steps 

Sustainability
the environment and how these will influence every decision that we make
Collaboration
The effect of combined inspiration, tools and techniques to use collaboration to your advantage 
Creativity & curiosity
Recognising these skills as a superpower 
Problem Solving  
The most sought-after skill required by employers today, how to maximise outcomes

Digital Tools
Experimenting with digital innovations as enablers to grow your portfolio and your skillset
Leadership
Navigating and understanding different leadership styles, finding yours, embracing it and being proud of it 
Entrepreneurship
Coming up with ideas, having the skills to evaluate them, and getting them ready for market 
Interviewing
Recognising your own potential and values in order to sell yourself confidently

Getting comfortable with failure as a key component of personal development
